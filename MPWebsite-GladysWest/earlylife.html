Gladys West
How She Changed All Our Lives - Early Life

The life that Gladys West led hadn't been an easy life. She lived in poverty, and she often worked at her parents' farm in Virginia. With her status as a black woman, it was difficult to get a good job, as her only options were either to continue working at the farm, or work at a tobacco company, neither of which she had desired. However, she was a dilligent student, allowing her to attend Virginia State University, majoring in mathematics.

But there was a problem. Her family couldn't pay for her tuition. There was a solution. Her state was giving a full scholarship to the two best students of the year. She studied harder than ever to get that scholarship, and in the end, she got it. She was going to college.
